## 🏀 SUMMER LEAGUE — LAS VEGAS, MONDAY JULY 13

**CORRECTION NOTE:** Full slate was 7 games — the headline performances cut across the board.

**Storyline — Meleek Thomas steals the day.** With all eyes on the top picks, it was Cavaliers undrafted rookie **Meleek Thomas** who put up the biggest number: **35 points (14-of-23 FG, 5-of-9 FT, 5 threes)** in a 90–73 win over Miami — tying a franchise SL record and coming within 7 of the all-time single-game mark. Jaxson Robinson added 13/6 for Cleveland.

**Storyline — Caleb Wilson comes back to earth.** After the record 35-point debut, the No. 4 pick cooled off: **~19 points** as the **Jazz beat the Bulls 80–63**. Utah's **Jonas Aidoo** led all scorers with 15 pts / 11 reb.

**Storyline — Kobe Johnson and the Hawks down Boston.** In a game that flew under the radar, **Atlanta Hawks 102, Boston Celtics 90** — **Kobe Johnson** erupted for **30 points (11-of-18 FG, 4 threes) + 7 reb / 4 ast / 2 stl** in 24 minutes; **Henri Veesaar** added 20. The Hawks dominated with a 16-0 mid-second-quarter run that Boston never recovered from.

**Will Riley goes for 32 — Dybantsa keeps rolling.** **Wizards 104, Kings 85**: Washington's Will Riley dropped **32 points** alongside No. 1 pick **AJ Dybantsa's 23/7/3stl/2blk** — the Wizards' best offensive display of the summer. Maxime Raynaud had a 20/12 for Sacramento in a losing effort.

**Mavericks top Grizzlies; Boozer remains impressive.** **Dallas 96, Memphis 88** — Cameron Boozer stayed consistent with **21 pts / 8 reb / 3 stl**, but Kaodirichi Akobundu-Ehiogu led Dallas with 16 pts and the Mavs held on.

**Suns edge Bucks in two-point thriller.** **Phoenix 77, Milwaukee 75** — Former Arizona teammates Koa Peat (PHX, 11 pts) and Brayden Burries (MIL) faced off; the Suns held on by two.

**Other Vegas results (July 13):**
- **Knicks 86, Pistons 75** — Jack Kayil 19, Dillon Jones 18 / 6 reb / 4 ast for New York's first Vegas win.
- **Cavaliers 90, Heat 73** — Meleek Thomas 35 (see above).

*Continuity — AJ Dybantsa watch:* The No. 1 pick has now averaged ~25 ppg across two games (27 debut, 23 tonight). Washington may shut him down after two games — so his Vegas cameo could be over. Will Riley, 32 tonight, is quietly the Wizards' most surprising SL performer.
