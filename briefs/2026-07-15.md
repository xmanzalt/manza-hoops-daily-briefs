## 🔴 BREAKING / TOP STORIES

- **SHAMS NARROWS LEBRON TO THREE: PHILADELPHIA, CLEVELAND, MIAMI.** The market finally tightened. All formal pitches are in, and ESPN's Shams Charania reports James is deciding between the **76ers, Cavaliers and Heat** — *"It's decision time right now for LeBron James."* Chris Haynes added that LeBron **"has all the information needed,"** meaning it can drop at any moment. Marc Stein reports **Golden State "has not relented"** — Joe Lacob is staying in it to the end.
  - *Why it matters:* Two weeks of everybody-is-in-it speculation just collapsed into a three-horse race. The Knicks/Celtics/Timberwolves names Rich Paul floated Monday look a lot more like leverage today.

- **THE VOICE-MEMO SAGA — OWNERS ARE RECORDING AUDIO PITCHES TO REACH LEBRON.** Shams revealed LeBron isn't taking in-person meetings, so **owners, team presidents and GMs are recording voice notes and sending them to Rich Paul**, who relays them. Golden State, Cleveland, Miami, Philadelphia and Minnesota have all reportedly done it. Paul's response when asked: *"I did not tell him that, but he found it out, and, you know, he had the information."*
  - *Why it matters:* The most absurd and most viral image of the offseason — billionaires leaving voicemails for a 41-year-old. It also tells you exactly where the leverage sits.

- **SIXERS SEND THEIR STARS AFTER HIM — MAXEY LEADING THE CHARGE.** ESPN's Dave McMenamin reported **Tyrese Maxey, Joel Embiid and Jaylen Brown** are all in direct contact with LeBron, with **Maxey leading**. Maxey is a fellow Klutch client who has trained with LeBron for years and calls him "big brother"; Embiid won 2024 Olympic gold with him; Brown got an unsolicited LeBron MVP endorsement in February.
  - *Why it matters:* Philly is the only suitor running a genuine player-led pitch — and its lead recruiter shares an agent with LeBron. Not a coincidence.

- **WIZARDS SHUT DOWN AJ DYBANTSA FOR THE REST OF SUMMER LEAGUE.** The No. 1 overall pick is done in Vegas after two games — **50 total points**, capped by a 23/7/3stl/2blk line vs. the Kings. Washington saw enough and is protecting the asset.
  - *Why it matters:* Called it yesterday. He was so good so fast the team pulled him. The Bulls beat the leaderless Wizards 99–87 Tuesday with Dybantsa on the bench.

- **ALPHA DIALLO SIGNS WITH DENVER — EUROLEAGUE'S BEST DEFENDER MAKES THE JUMP.** The **EuroLeague Defender of the Year** agreed to a **1-year, $1.4M minimum deal** with the Nuggets. Averaged 11.9 pts / 4.4 reb / 1.3 stl for Monaco while leading the league in steals. He **left millions on the table**, exercising an NBA exit clause in his Dubai Basketball contract to make the jump at 29.
  - *Why it matters:* Denver ranked 21st and 22nd in defensive rating the last two seasons. This is a free, low-risk swing at exactly their problem — and a guy who chose the NBA over the money.

## 🏀 SUMMER LEAGUE — LAS VEGAS, TUESDAY JULY 14 (DAY 6)

Six games. A second-round pick stole the show and Egor Demin made a statement.

**Nuggets 106, Thunder 103 — Trevon Brazile ERUPTS for 32.** Denver's No. 35 pick lit it up: **32 pts (11-of-19 FG, 6-of-12 from three), 6 reb, 1 blk** to win a wire-to-wire thriller. **Brooks Stirtz** kept OKC alive with 22 pts / 6 ast. A second-rounder outscoring everyone on the floor in a close game is what Summer League is made for.

**Nets 115, Kings 83 — Egor Demin is DIFFERENT.** Brooklyn's 2025 No. 8 pick posted **22 pts, 8 ast, 4 stl — zero turnovers**, with 16 of those points inside the first 10 minutes. The Nets forced **28 Kings turnovers** and scored **41 points off them**. Elijah Sharp had 23 and 4 steals for Sacramento in defeat.

**Battle of Los Angeles — Lakers 99, Clippers 85.** **Cameron Carr** led all scorers with **23**; **Christian Manon** added a **17-point, 11-rebound** double-double. Kobe Sanders paced the Clippers with 18/6.

**Rockets 90, 76ers 64 — the blowout of the night.** Houston smothered Philadelphia, holding the Sixers to **31.1% from the field and 5-of-30 (16.7%) from three**. **Isaiah Crawford** had 19/6; Labaron Philon Jr. led Philly with 17. Rockets move to 2–1.

**Grizzlies 106, Warriors 85.** **Javon Small** ran the show with **26 pts (5-of-8 from three) / 7 ast**. **Cameron Boozer had a quiet night by his standards — 12 pts on 4-of-12, 7 reb in 26 min.** Golden State's **Brendan Hausen** led his side with 20 (6-of-8 from three) and **Yaxel Lendeborg** added 15/9.

**Bulls 99, Wizards 87.** **Jalen Sellers** led Chicago with **24 pts / 3 stl**. Chris Livingston had 21 for a Wizards team playing without Dybantsa.

*Continuity — the Dybantsa watch is closed.* Two games, 50 points, shut down. **Boozer is now the top lottery pick still playing**, and after Tuesday's 12-point night, the story of the summer is increasingly the guys nobody drafted high: Brazile (32), Demin (22/8/4), Meleek Thomas (35 on Jul 13), Will Riley (32 on Jul 13).
