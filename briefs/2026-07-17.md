## 🔴 BREAKING / TOP STORIES

- **LEBRON WENT TO FANATICS FEST AND ANNOUNCED… NOTHING.** The most anticipated day of the offseason came and went. LeBron recorded *Mind the Game* live with Tyrese Haliburton at the Javits Center, drank wine on stage, laughed off recruiting pitches from the crowd — and never revealed a team. When Haliburton asked directly whether a decision still had to be made, LeBron deflected: *"We literally talked about this in the back."* OutKick called it **"the ultimate nothing-burger."**
  - *Why it matters:* Marc Stein floated this as the reveal stage. Every reporter in basketball was in the building. He gave them a wine glass and a shrug. The decision is still coming — but the entire league just got trolled for a full news cycle.

- **THE "LAST YEAR OR TWO" LINE IS THE REAL STORY.** Buried in the non-announcement: LeBron openly framed this as choosing where to spend **"the last few years, or the last year, or the last two years"** — answering an **11-year-old's** question, not a reporter's. He said he's weighing **"what best fits me as a player, what best fits me as a person, and what best fits my happiness, and also my family."** He also called LA **"my former team"** outright.
  - *Why it matters:* First time he's publicly put a clock on himself. And note what's *missing* from that criteria list: winning. If happiness and family are the deciders, the math points somewhere very different than Miami.

- **RICH PAUL PUBLICLY BREAKS WITH SHAMS OVER THE VOICE-NOTE STORY.** ESPN's Shams Charania reported that because LeBron has declined formal meetings, teams — **Cavaliers, Heat, Warriors, Timberwolves, 76ers** — are recruiting him by sending **voice notes through agent Rich Paul.** Paul went on *NBA Today* and distanced himself from the leak: **"I did not tell him that, but he found it out, and, you know, he had the information."**
  - *Why it matters:* Rare daylight between the league's biggest agent and its biggest reporter. Also: five franchises are reduced to leaving voicemails for LeBron James. Most 2026 sentence in basketball.

- **BOB MYERS WENT ON RICH PAUL'S OWN PODCAST TO PITCH THE SIXERS.** Philadelphia ownership president **Bob Myers** appeared on *Game Over with Max Kellerman and Rich Paul* — the agent's own show — and pitched live on air: if LeBron wants to win, **"you can win here, in Philadelphia."**
  - *Why it matters:* Sam Amick reportedly cut Philly from the list two days ago. Their response was to walk into the agent's living room and pitch anyway. That's not a team that thinks it's out.

- **GRIZZLIES HOLD HAWKS TO TWO POINTS IN A QUARTER — A SUMMER LEAGUE RECORD.** Memphis opened **32–2**, shooting 13-of-17 while Atlanta went **1-of-13** and didn't score until three minutes were left in the period. Final: **Grizzlies 96, Hawks 64.** Cameron Boozer finally erupted: **24 pts on 10-of-13, 7 reb, 3 ast.**
  - *Why it matters:* A record-low quarter, a box score that doesn't look real, and the No. 3 pick's breakout landing on the exact night he needed it. Best pure highlight content of the day.

## 🏀 SUMMER LEAGUE — LAS VEGAS, THURSDAY JULY 16 (DAY 8 / QUARTERFINALS)

Seven games, elimination day. **Lakers, Grizzlies, Rockets and Warriors** advance to the semifinals.

**Grizzlies 96, Hawks 64 — Boozer ERUPTS: 24 pts / 7 reb / 3 ast (10-of-13 FG).** The **32–2 first quarter** is a **Summer League record low.** Atlanta shot 1-of-13 and trailed by as many as 36. Isaiah Wong led the Hawks with 20. The No. 3 pick's best Vegas night by a mile, after a slow start to the tournament.

**Lakers 105, Bulls 82 — six in double figures.** Balanced LA attack overcame **Caleb Wilson (21 pts, 8 reb)**, who needed only three quarters to get there. Chicago's lottery pick was the best player on the floor and lost by 23. Lakers finish as the **1 seed, undefeated.**

**Warriors 87, Knicks 77 — Cryer leads, Lendeborg fills the sheet.** **LJ Cryer** hit five threes for **17 pts**; **Graham Ike** added 15 and 9; **Malevy Leons** and **Lajae Jones** had 13 each. No. 11 pick **Yaxel Lendeborg** went quiet-but-useful: **3 pts, 6 reb, 6 ast.** Knicks' **Jaden Akins** led NY with 21 off the bench. Will Richard sat; Jones got his first Vegas start.

**Rockets 100, Nets 83 — Thornton and Newton take over.** **Bruce Thornton** (No. 31 pick) went **23 pts, 4 reb, 2 ast, 2 stl**; **Tristen Newton** added **20 pts, 6 reb, 2 stl.** Brooklyn's **Egor Demin** kept it interesting with 21 (5-of-8 from three) but the Nets go home.

**Mavericks 97, Thunder 87 — de Larrea's 14 assists.** **Sergio de Larrea** dished **14 assists** — the passing performance of the entire tournament — as Dallas won without Morez Johnson Jr. Dallas does **not** advance.

**Raptors 99, Heat 90 — Graves delivers, but Toronto is OUT.** No. 19 pick **Allen Graves** put up **23 pts, 6 reb, 4 ast**, **Seth Lundy** added 19, and Toronto finished **3–1** — *not enough to crack the top four.* Miami eliminated too.

**Trail Blazers 108, Nuggets 101 — depth wins it.** Seven Portland players scored between 11 and 16 in a total team effort. Their win is what mathematically clinched Golden State's semifinal spot.
