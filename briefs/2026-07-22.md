- **PAUL'S ADVICE TO LEBRON: "DON'T JUST VALUE THE PAPER."** In the same episode, Paul framed the whole decision as pressure-free: *"This is the first time LeBron James is making a decision pressure free… This is strictly for his happiness."* His counsel: *"Don't just value the paper. Because the paper in July is different than when you get into training camp, when you get into January."*
  - *Why it matters:* Translation — money and roster on paper aren't the deciding factor. That reads as fit/happiness over a max contract, which keeps Cleveland, Miami, and Philadelphia (and a Warriors long-shot) all realistically alive.

- **REPORTS SAY A DECISION IS "NEAR" — BUT HIS CAMP DISAGREES.** Multiple outlets (Bleacher Report, Yahoo) reported momentum is building and James could decide "in the coming days," even "within 48 hours." Bleacher Report also noted James **still wasn't ready to choose on Tuesday** and there's **no "elaborate rollout plan."**
  - *Why it matters:* Classic offseason tension — media manufacturing a clock while the agent openly says there isn't one. Manza can play the gap: everyone's guessing, the one guy who knows says nobody knows.

- **THE FINALIST BOARD (per multiple reports):** Cleveland Cavaliers, Miami Heat, Philadelphia 76ers, and Golden State Warriors are the tabbed favorites, with Denver and Minnesota floated as outside possibilities. Cleveland has the Harden big-three pitch; Miami has Giannis and the Polymarket edge; Philly has Jaylen Brown.
  - *Why it matters:* The board hasn't changed — it just keeps getting re-reported. Until Shams tweets a team, treat all of it as the same three-plus-one field.
